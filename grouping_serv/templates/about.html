Iâ€™m just a high school student in KCIS having a hobby of coding. I first discover my hobby in grade eight, and work on coding problems and projects. I found a problem in real life grouping algorithms. I noticed that most man-made grouping algorithms are not optimized. There are these types of algorithms in common:

Free group:
In this case, everyone is allowed to choose their groupmates by themselves. Since everyone want to be happy, they choose according to their favorites. However, this caused several groups to be happy while the leftover remainings are unhappy.

Randomized group In this case, the happiness of each group is random, and unable to make the unhappiest group happier.

When I was in elementary school, I used to be leftover almost everytime in making groups. I observed the common grouping algorithms and found weakness of each. Thus, I create this online app in approach to optimize grouping.

The algorithm

The website collects data of favored and unfavored groups from each classroom user

and form a datastructure: [user, [[favored, []], unfavored, []]]

the program calculate the all possibility of grouping of every member in a class

Happiness calculating algorithm

In each group, if one person is grouped with his/her favored person, overall happiness of the group +1,

if grouped with unfavored person, overall happiness of the group -1.

The difference of the most happy group and the most unhappy group is measured in every cases,

The algorithm finally randomly select the case with the happiest least happy group.
